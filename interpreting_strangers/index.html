Appendix

A Guide to Generating Images from Prompts

The images in Interpreting Strangers were created using AI and language—nothing more than a few well-crafted prompts. These prompts served as the seed, the intention, the mood. The result? Expressive black-and-white images that look like collage, photography, or dreams in visual form.

This guide will show you how to generate similar images using freely available tools and your own creativity.

What Is a Prompt?

A prompt is a short text input used to guide AI image generation. It can be as simple as:

"Black-and-white portrait of a woman sitting on a park bench, hands folded, melancholy expression."

Or more poetic:

"She waits with the stillness of old letters, rain behind her, hope pressed like a flower in a closed book."

Tools You Can Use

Here are some beginner-friendly platforms to generate AI images:

1. Bing Image Creator (DALL·E 3) -- Free

  • Bing Image Creator
  • Great for photo-style and painterly images
  • Accepts poetic or narrative prompts

2. Leonardo AI -- Free & Paid

  • leonardo.ai
  • Excellent for stylistic variety, including collage-like effects
  • Choose from multiple models and tweak details

3. NightCafe -- Free & Paid

  • nightcafe.studio
  • Easy-to-use with many style presets

4. Adobe Firefly -- Free with Adobe Account

  • firefly.adobe.com
  • Reliable, with options for artistic or photographic style

Prompt Writing Tips

Keep It Focused

Mention 2–3 clear subjects or elements

"Old man, chessboard, city window"

Include Mood or Emotion

"Soft sadness, foggy light, alone but not lonely"

Specify Style (optional)

"Collage style, black and white, high contrast"
"Painterly, dreamy, soft shadows"

Think Like a Director

Where is the scene set? Indoors or out? Close-up or distant?

What is the subject doing? What is the lighting?

Mix Literal and Poetic

Combine realism with feeling:

"Two teenagers laughing in the street, wind in their hair, joy catching like wildfire"

Examples of Prompts

  • "Woman in party dress, sitting alone on a wooden chair, looking out a window. Black and white, soft focus."
  • "Man at a kitchen table with cards and cheese. Light from a single window. Shadowy past implied."
  • "Little girl with hands folded, quiet but aware, as if waiting for a secret to arrive."

Adding Collage Elements

To create a collage look:

  • Include words like "cut paper," "collage," "layered texture," or "torn edges"
  • Use prompts like:
"Black-and-white digital collage of a woman, tree branches, old letters, and a clock face"

Tips for Saving and Using Images

  • Most generators allow you to download images in JPG or PNG format.
  • Resize to 5×8 inches at 300dpi for book inserts.
  • Convert color to grayscale for black-and-white printing (Photoshop, Preview, Canva).

Creative Ideas

  • Use prompts as writing starters or journaling cues.
  • Create a visual moodboard for your story or character.
  • Make a personal oracle deck or digital art series.
  • Teach students creative writing and visual interpretation through prompt play.

Final Thought

The AI doesn't do the magic. You do.

The prompt is your whisper, your brushstroke, your breath.

You don't need to be an artist. You just need curiosity.

Now—go make something beautiful.
